    Our Story

    Shake Shack sprouted from a hot dog cart in Madison Square Park in Manhattan created by Danny Meyer’s Union Square Hospitality Group (USHG) to support the Madison Square Park Conservancy’s first art installation. The cart was quite the success, with Shack Fans lined up daily for three summers.

    IN IT TO PIN IT

    Check out what inspires us everyday on Pinterest.

    In 2004, USHG won the bid to open a permanent kiosk in the Park: Shake Shack was born. This modern day “roadside” burger stand serves up the most delicious burgers, hot dogs, frozen custard, shakes, beer, wine and more. An instant neighborhood fixture, Shake Shack welcomed people from all over the city, country and world who gathered together to enjoy fresh, simple, high-quality versions of the classics in a majestic setting.

    The rest, as they say, is burger history.

    Latest News

    • Miami, The Sequel

      July 15th we opened our second Sunshine State Shack in Coral Gables just a burger’s throw from the University of Miami. With Shake Shack in town it’s gonna be a whole new U! Go ’CANES!

    • Did Someone Say Bacon?!!

      It’s SmokeShack time! Cheeseburger topped with all-natural applewood smoked bacon, chopped cherry pepper & ShackSauce. Available at US Shacks only, no ballparks. You’re bacon me crazy!

    • Beautiful Beach

      Our South Beach Shack loves the beach. That’s why 5% of sales from our Vice Crispy Treat concrete support the Environmental Coalition of Miami & the Beaches.

    • School’s Now in Session

      The hallowed halls of Yale are now filled with ShackBurgers and frozen custard with Shake Shack taking root in The Elm City. Classes in deliciousness began September 13th. Glad to meet ya, New Haven!

    • Custard for the Soul

      Our partnership with Miami Children’s Hospital Foundation is moving to our brand new Coral Gables Shack. The Shack team will continue to visit these awesome kids with monthly frozen custard socials and smiles.

    • Ein Prosit!

      From 10/12-10/21, we raised our steins at our annual Shacktoberfest. The celebration’s limited time menu, featuring Schaller & Weber brats and shakes in traditional German flavors, had us all saying “Guten Appetit!”

    • Let the Sun Shine

      We’re in a Long Island State of Mind! Our very first LI Shack made its home in Westbury on November 10th. Its solar panel covered roof makes this our greenest Shack yet.

    • So Long Sandy, Let’s Rally!

      Our guests are truly the best! From 11/4 – 11/11, the “Rally Shake” raised $11,111 for Superstorm Sandy relief efforts at the American Red Cross.

    • Autumn Just Got Sweeter

      Our PUMPKIN PIE, OH MY! Concrete is back. Until Dec. 2nd, cozy up to Fall with our vanilla frozen custard blended with a slice of pumpkin pie. Taste for yourself why this is our most popular concrete, ever!

    Latest Events

    • Bring on the Bash

      Shake Shack was proud to take part in NYC’s Burger Bash on October 12th, supporting hunger relief. Burger lovers tried our brand-new bacon-topped SmokeShack burger and Pumpkin Pie, Oh My Concrete. As Bash host Rachael Ray would say, “Yum-o!”

    • A Shack-able Feast

      We joined top local chefs at FEASTIVAL to benefit the Philadelphia Live Arts and Philly Fringe. On Sep. 12th, we raised a glass of Shack Red to all things innovative and daring in the Philly culinary and art world.

    • Small Town in the Big Apple

      New York City may be big, but Battery Park City locals know it’s all about the community. Our BPC Shack provided SHACKwiches (aka tasty frozen custard sandwiched between whoopie pies) alongside neighborhood restaurants at the BPC Block Party on Sep. 12th.

    • Picking a Fight With Hunger

      Top chefs battled it out to support DC Central Kitchen on Oct. 8th at Capital Food Fight, hosted by Anthony Bourdain and José Andrés. The DC team were on hand to dish out signature items while the cooking war raged onstage.

    • Eat, Drink, and Give to Charity

      The Westport Shack were breaking it down alongside celebrity chefs and musicians like Gregg Allman at the Greenwich Food + Wine Festival on Oct. 6th. All proceeds benefited Paul Newman’s The Hole in the Wall Gang Camp. Cheers to that!

    • Bow Wow Wow

      Our Miami team got down with the dogs at Sit. Stay. Love., an adoption event held at the local Anthropologie Sept. 17th. Guests met adorable pups from the Humane Society, while the dogs met (and devoured) tasty Pooch-inis straight from the Shack.

    • So Good You Can Taste It

      The spotlight was on local businesses at Taste of DC (10/6) and Taste of the Beach in Miami (9/29-9/30). Our Shacks were on hand to serve up signature ShackBurgers and special frozen custard like Maple Bacon Banan-za. Keepin’ it local!

    • Shack or Treat

      Boo! No need to get scared – it’s just Shackenstein frozen custard and ShackBurgers at the Monster Masquerade. We hit up Zoo Miami for this Halloween fest on 10/19.

    • Go ‘Canes!

      Football season’s in full swing at UM. We got pumped up with pregame parties before Florida State (10/20) and Virginia Tech (11/1). Free U-Malt concretes? Score!

    • Let’s Get Trivial

      Frozen custard: it’s good for the brain! Our BK Shack is scooping Brooklyn Blackout custard at 826NYC’s Trivia Night with John Hodgman on 11/20, benefitting free writing programs for students.

    Stand for something good

    We are the world! We love our planet and all its inhabitants. We’re working hard to lessen our footprint, and promise to keep pushing to make important new strides. We’re committed to extending hospitality beyond the four walls of each Shack into the surrounding community. Thank you for standing with us as we look to the future.

    Here's a glimpse of what's going on now:

    • Good Ingredients
    • Good N' Green
    • Good Bones
    • Good Neighbors

    • Good Ingredients

      100% all-natural Angus beef, vegetarian fed, humanely raised and source verified. No hormones or antibiotics – EVER. We pride ourselves on sourcing incredible ingredients from like-minded artisanal producers.

    • Good N' Green

      100% of our electric usage is offset through wind power and Renewable Energy Certificates. Bottles, cardboard, and plastics are recycled. Our cooking oil is reused to produce clean energy and our kitchen food is composted.

    • Good Bones

      Each Shack is constructed from recycled and sustainable materials. Tabletops sourced from reclaimed bowling alley lanes, walls made of reclaimed antique barn wood siding, booths feature lumber certified by the Forestry Stewardship Council. We use energy efficient kitchen equipment and employ LED lighting throughout.

    • Good Neighbors

      We’re all about our hood! We strive to be the best employers and citizens of each community we call home. Whether partnering with Share Our Strength or working with local charitable organizations to donate 5% of sales of a specific concrete – the Shack is committed to giving back.
