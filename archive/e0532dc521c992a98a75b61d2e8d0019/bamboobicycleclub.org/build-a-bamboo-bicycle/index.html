The Bamboo Bicycle Club Workshop

Tucked away in an East London warehouse, the Bamboo Bicycle Club runs workshops allowing people to build bamboo bicycle frames safely, drawing on all the knowledge and experience of Club members and founders.

The workshops are run over a weekend, with days starting at 8am and usually wrapping up at around 5-6pm. You’ll definitely need all 10 hours on the Saturday as there is a lot to do!

You can just build a bamboo frame, or we can source parts for you so you can start the assembly process immediately and get your new bike on the road!

Pre-workshop: Before the course starts you’ll need complete our joining questionnaire, providing us with all the details we need to create a CAD model of your frame, personalised for your physical geometry – you’ll then be able to crack straight on with build when you arrive!

Saturday:  By far the busiest day of the build, you’ll set up your jig, select bamboo tubes and mitre them to fit the steel and cromoly installations. Everything gets tacked into place ready for binding the next day

Sunday: Focusing on binding and creating all the joints. This is normally finished by lunchtime, we then take a extended lunch break and return to start taking the finished frames of the jig.

Post-Workshop: We won’t be going anywhere! – by this point, you’ll fully inaugurated members of the Bamboo Bicycle Club, and we’re here to help you get the best out of your new bikes. So, if you need to book-in to use the workshop & tools, need some advice on parts, or if you only want to cruise by and show us how swish your new wheel-set is, then get in touch!
